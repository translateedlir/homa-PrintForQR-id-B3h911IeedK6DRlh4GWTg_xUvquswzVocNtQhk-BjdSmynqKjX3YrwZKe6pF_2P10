Article 6: Legal Consequences of the Contract

6-1. The lessee cannot use the object of lease for any purpose other than residential purpose and act against custom, 6-2. The lessee has the right to use the object of lease as supervision (has no right to transfer the premise to any third party), in case of violation, the lessee will be responsible for all damages incurred, 6-3. Lessee undertakes to pay all water, electricity, telephone charges and maintenance costs as well as urban wastewater treatment against receipt at the time of evacuation, 6-4. All general and repairs costs such as, installation and utilization of air conditioner, heating center, cooler, elevator, water network, electricity and gas shall be paid by lessors and partial expenses rests with lessee, and types of expenses and their value are determined by custom. 6-5. Estate tax, basic repairs, municipality charges and tax shall be paid by lessors. Business charges, taxable income (commercial and administrative) of Finance Department, insurance premium of personnel with Social Security Organization shall be paid by lessee, 6-6. Regarding commercial places, no right brings for lessee as key money or business right and the lessee has no claim in this regard, and is committed to vacate the object of lease by termination of the contract 6-7. The lessee undertakes to evacuate the premise in good condition as the same of delivery against receipt, and in case of damages incurred to the object of lease, the lessee is committed to compensate all damages 6-8. If the lessee does not evacuate the object of lease completely or by any reason does not deliver the premise on due time, shall pay sum of Rls. 20,000,000/- as damage per day after termination date of the contract as fee of possession to the lessor, and also this sum can be deducted from the deposited sum to the lessor. Anyway, payment of this value does not rebar the right of referring to the judicial authorities to follow evacuation of the object of lease by the lessor or agent of real estate, 6-9. The lessor is entitled to return interest- free amount on expiry date otherwise, she shall pay sum of Rls. 2,000,000/- to the other party for any one-day delay as the compensation of the incurred losses. It also would not be an obstacle to pursuit the matters by judicial authorities, 6-10. All options were waived except trickery, 6-11. This contract will be under Civil Code and Act on Relationship of Lessee and Lessors enacted in 1997, 6-12. This contract would not be valid without seal and signature of real estate agent, 6-13. The lessee undertakes to observe all moral and Islamic prestige in duration of the contract and has no right to use the object of lease as pyramid companies. 6-14. If any party tends to cancel the contract before expiry date, he/ she shall inform the matters one month in advance and pay one- month rental as compensation, 6-15. Original title deed and related papers which indicate the ownership were observed by parties to the contract and they confessed this fact by signing the copies. 6-16. Any further agreement made between the parties and are not mentioned above shall be mentioned in the following as remarks and signed by parties.
